About Me

Hi, I'm Connor Forsythe, a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University. My research focuses on understanding the policy-related impacts of extended vehicle life, electric vehicle adoption, and transportation network companies. I completed my B.S. in Systems Engineering at The George Washington University, where I graduated summa cum laude. I have a strong background in programming, with proficiency in Python, MATLAB, Stata, R, and Julia. Throughout my academic career, I've actively participated in both research and teaching roles, serving as a Graduate Research and Teaching Assistant at CMU and as an Undergraduate Research Assistant at GWU. I'm currently working on several research projects that explore various aspects of transportation and policy. In my free time, I enjoy watching movies as well as listening to and playing music. Feel free to connect with me through connorforsythe@cmu.edu.
